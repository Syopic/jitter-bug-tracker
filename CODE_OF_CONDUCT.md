# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for everyone participating in the Jitter bug tracker. We pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community and game improvement
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Providing detailed and accurate bug reports
- Being patient with developers and other community members
- Helping others when possible

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, trolling, or personal attacks
- Insulting or derogatory comments
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Spam, off-topic discussions, or promotional content
- Deliberately posting false or misleading information
- Sharing game exploits or vulnerabilities publicly before they are fixed
- Using the bug tracker for purposes other than game improvement
- Any other conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Reporting Issues

### Bug Reports and Feature Requests

When reporting bugs or requesting features:

- Provide accurate and complete information
- Be clear and concise in your descriptions
- Avoid duplicate submissions by searching existing issues first
- Stay on topic and avoid off-topic discussions
- Update your issues with new information if it becomes available

### Violations of Code of Conduct

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. Flagging the inappropriate content (if possible)
2. Contacting the repository maintainers through GitHub
3. Providing details about the incident

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

## Enforcement

### Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

### Actions We May Take

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject:

- Comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct
- Users who engage in unacceptable behavior

### Enforcement Guidelines

Violations may result in:

1. **Warning** - A private or public warning about the behavior
2. **Temporary Ban** - Temporary restriction from participating
3. **Permanent Ban** - Permanent removal from the community

The severity of the action will depend on the nature and frequency of the violation.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces related to the Jitter bug tracker, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Any communication that references this project
- Any representation of the project in public spaces

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted from common open source community guidelines and best practices.

## Questions

If you have questions about this Code of Conduct, please open an issue or contact the project maintainers.

---

By participating in the Jitter bug tracker, you agree to abide by this Code of Conduct. Thank you for helping us maintain a positive and productive community!
